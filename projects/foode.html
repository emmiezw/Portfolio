WHAT'S FOODE

A communication platform that connects restaurant owners and customers with honest and private feedbacks

Food-e is an entrepreneur project based in University of Michigan.

Food-e bridges restaurant owners and customers with high-quality private reviews. Customers can provide anonymous honest feedbacks to obtain rewards, while restaurant owners can improve based on these feedback. A healthy loop like this will potentially incentivize more cutomer returns and maintain good customer relationships in the long-run.

Our team scope for this project is to build the mobile app for the customer end.


MY ROLES & AGILE DEVELOPMENT

ROAD TO MINIMUM VIABLE PRODUCT

User research & initial concept testing

In-Restaurant Observation & Interview

  • We created a in-store restaurant feedback-gathering google form (the method that restaurants are currently using).
  • We observed how 6 users interact with web forms in our partner restaurant and interviewed to understand their motivations.

Offsite Interview & Concept Testing

  • After In-Restaurant user research, we gathered preliminary information on user persona, and brainstormed 6 ideas accordingly.
  • We conducted 6 more user interview sessions to discuss these 6 ideas respectively and draw more insights.

After the two kinds of user research, we were able to identify the key persona for our customers. Based on user’s comments on different initial concepts we were able to draw multiple insights for low-fi prototyping.

Low-fidelity prototyping

To learn fast and move fast, we started development with a low fidelity prototype, and learn along each sprints to align deliverables with product owner expectations and user needs.

Minimal Viable Product

With sessions of mini testing and sprint reviews, we managed to grow deep understanding of both technical side and product design side of Food-e, and accumulated a huge product backlog.

During sprint planning, we estimate efforts and help determine acceptance criteria to help product owner prioritize features.

In the first half of 4th sprint, we were able to deliver our MVP. There are 5 key features implemented: Sign In/Sign Up, Explore Restaurants, Leave Feedbacks, Redeem Coupons, Edit Profile.

TESTING

In the fourth sprint, we conducted thorough usability testing with the MVP. Along with major usability problems, we discovered a couple of product challenges that remained unsolved in our MVP.

Journey Mapping & Design Opportunities

I created a Journey Map and identified 3 major design opportunities based on the usability testing findings. Figuring our these three “How Might We”s would be high impact for Food-e’s to meet its value proposition and obtain growth as an early stage entrepreneur product.

  • Challenge 1: (Return Users)How might we incentivize user to leave quality feedbacks?
  • Challenge 2: (Royal Users)How might we increase long-term user engagemnt?

CHALLENGE 1: INCENTIVIZE QUALITY FEEDBACK

How might we incentivize user to leave quality feedbacks?

/User tends to finish fast with low quality
During user testing, we noticed that user tends to skip text fields at the end, which prevent the restaurants from getting quality, detailed feedbacks. Therefore, my design focused on nudging customers to put in more contents with improved usability.

/User is lack of internal drive
Another finding is that users are currently driven by external motivations, coupons. To create internal motivations of “helping the restaurant”, my design focused on highlighting that they are helping local business. Also, this is related to Challenge 3.

Improvement 1: Diversify incentives for different types of users

I split the current “review” feature into two different features for two types of users. User can behave differently based on their thoughts on specific restaurants, and use these two features differently.

Improvement 2: Improve core experiences for survey and suggestion

MVP testing revealed a lot of usability issues in existing UI. When dividing review into “Survey” and “Suggestion”, I improved the core experience with these insights.

The main focus is to show user status and highlight primary actions with clear visual cues.

CHALLENGE 2: GAIN LONG TERM ENGAGEMENT

How might we increase long-term user engagemnt?

In the process of developing MVP, we tested out multiple engagement methods including badge system and level system.

However, after the final usability testing, we reached the conclusion that such social-focused incentives have small impacts on motivating our users because tall the reviews are private (visible only to user and the restaurant owner).

In order to create a more dynamic and long-lasting Food-e community that drives user to constantly leave new suggestions and pursue more than just coupons, I designed for two major improvements.

Improvement 1: Create constant engagement with invitation feature

Improvement 2: Create restaurant royalty by more communication features
